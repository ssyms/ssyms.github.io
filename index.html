What I Do
ABC: Always Be Coding
Hi, I'm Sarada. I'm currently pursuing a double major in computer engineering and computer science with a minor in Business Administration at Northeastern University in Boston. I started programming my first semester of college, and since then I've been exploring the field of computer science through research, projects (see below), hackathons, and sites like TopCoder and OpenHatch. Over the summer of 2015, I had the opportunity to attend Make School to learn about entrepreneurship and app development. I've also created apps in iOS and Android, and I am currently developing a web application. Due to an interest in security, I've also started reading about and practicing pen testing on Hack This Site.

Programming Languages: Python (preferred), Swift, C, C++

Skills: OpenCl, CUDA, Matlab, Microsoft Suite, Adobe Photoshop, InDesign, Solidworks, AutoCAD, Xcode, App Inventor, Parse

Relevant Extracurriculars: Northeastern University Computer Architecture Research Lab (Researcher), Embark: Northeastern's Undergradute Engineering Research Journal, Society of Women Engineers (SAC Representative), Enabling Engineering, Engineers without Borders

what i'm working on
VR Monitoring iOS and Web App
Currently, I'm working with a small group to design a product that will help physical therapists monitor the progress of patients remotely, particularly children who use Xbox Kinect games in their therapy. This includes collecting data on heart rates and movement using Arduino sensors and storing that data in Parse. I'm designing an iOS application for patients and a web application for therapists.


Parallel Programming Research
Since 2014, I have been researching parallel computing with a focus on CUDA and OpenCL with the Northeastern University Computer Architecture Research Group (NUCAR). Currently, I am optimizing Polybench suite programs and implementing prefetch strategies. I have had the chance to work with and learn from doctoral students Fanny Nina Paravecino and Leiming Yu and principal investigator Prof. David Kaeli.


Bites Log
My first app in iOS, Bites Log uses the Foursquare API to find nearby restaurants and allows users to save locations to their phone. They can then email a list of places (including addresses) to friends and family. Over the course of this project, I had the chance to work with JSON objects and the Swift programming language. This app was developed during my summer at Make School in San Francisco. Now available on the App Store.


Water Distribution System
I joined the Northeastern chapter of Engineers without Borders my freshman year and started work with the Uganda project, a water distribution system for the town of Bbanda. Last year, I analyzed and researched the viability of a solar powered pump system. This year I have been researching local NGOs and working on fundraising proposals.


Major Class Projects
Sumo Robot: Designed a sumo robot using Arduino devices and household items. The robot later came in first in a tournament against 17 other robots.
WiiMote Controlled Robotic Arm: Using an FPGA and concepts such as PWM, GPIO, and memory mapped IO, programmed a robotic arm to follow the commands of a WiiMote.
